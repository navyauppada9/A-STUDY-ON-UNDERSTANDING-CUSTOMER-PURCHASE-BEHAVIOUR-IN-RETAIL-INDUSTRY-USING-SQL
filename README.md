# Customer Purchase Behaviour Analysis in the Retail Industry Using SQL

## Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing customer purchase behaviour in the retail industry using SQL and Power BI. The objective was to transform raw retail transaction data into meaningful business insights by identifying purchasing patterns, customer preferences, product performance, and revenue trends.

The dataset was initially cleaned and prepared in Microsoft Excel to ensure consistency and data quality. The cleaned dataset was then imported into MySQL Workbench, where SQL queries were used to perform exploratory and business-focused analysis. Finally, Power BI was utilized to create interactive dashboards and visualizations that effectively communicated the findings.

## Objectives

* Analyze customer purchasing behaviour and spending patterns.
* Identify top-performing product categories and brands.
* Evaluate customer segment performance and contribution to revenue.
* Examine monthly sales trends and revenue growth.
* Understand customer preferences for shipping and payment methods.
* Analyze product ratings and customer satisfaction levels.
* Generate actionable business insights through SQL-based analysis and dashboard reporting.

## Tools & Technologies Used

* Microsoft Excel (Data Cleaning & Preparation)
* MySQL Workbench (Data Analysis & Query Execution)
* SQL
* Power BI (Data Visualization & Dashboard Development)

## Analysis Performed

The analysis was conducted using SQL queries to answer key business questions related to customer behaviour and retail performance. Areas analyzed include:

* Revenue generated by different product categories.
* Average purchase amount across countries.
* Monthly revenue trends and seasonal performance.
* Customer segment contribution to sales and transactions.
* Shipping method preferences and purchasing patterns.
* Product rating and review analysis.
* Revenue generated through different payment methods.
* Order status and fulfillment performance.
* Customer distribution based on gender and product preferences.
* Brand-wise sales performance and order volume.

Power BI dashboards were then developed to visualize these insights through charts, KPIs, and interactive reports, enabling easier interpretation of business performance.

## Power BI Dashboard 

An interactive Power BI dashboard was developed to visualize key insights generated through SQL analysis.

* Analyzed revenue by product category and country.
* Monitored monthly sales trends and revenue performance.
* Evaluated customer segments based on transactions and revenue.
* Assessed brand-wise sales and product rating performance.
* Compared payment methods and shipping preferences.
* Examined order status distribution and fulfillment performance.
* Analyzed customer demographics and product preferences.
* Utilized interactive filters and visualizations for efficient data exploration and decision-making.

## Key Findings

* Electronics emerged as the highest revenue-generating product category, outperforming Grocery and Clothing.
* Germany recorded the highest average purchase amount among all countries analyzed, indicating stronger customer spending behaviour.
* November 2023 generated the highest monthly revenue, suggesting the impact of seasonal demand, promotions, or holiday-related purchases.
* Regular customers contributed the highest revenue and transaction volume, while Premium customers contributed the least.
* Same-Day Shipping recorded the highest number of transactions and purchases, highlighting a strong customer preference for faster delivery services.
* Television achieved the highest average product rating and review count, reflecting strong customer satisfaction and engagement.
* Cash emerged as the leading revenue-generating payment method, surpassing Credit Card and Debit Card transactions.
* Nestlé ranked first in brand-wise sales performance, generating over ₹42 million in revenue from 2,975 orders, followed by Sony and Samsung.
* Product, customer, and transaction analyses revealed clear patterns that can support targeted marketing, inventory planning, and customer retention strategies.

## Conclusion

This project demonstrates the practical application of SQL in analyzing retail transaction data and extracting valuable business insights. By combining data cleaning in Excel, analytical querying in MySQL Workbench, and visualization in Power BI, the project successfully identified customer purchasing patterns, sales trends, product performance, and operational preferences. The findings provide a data-driven foundation for improving business decisions, enhancing customer experience, and optimizing retail performance.
